Genesis of Organizational Forms

Mixed-Methods Research | International Research | Foundational Research

I take pride in the PhD-level sociological research trainings I received at the University of Chicago, where I learned to do robust and rigorous research.

This project aims to investigate how new organizational forms are generated. The research was originally intended to serve as the first chapter of my dissertation, but later became a stand-alone project. In it, I deployed a variety of research methods involving in-depth, remote, semi-structured interviews and qualitative coding, surveys and statistical analysis and modeling, as well as web crawling to collect data. All of these methods are to serve one goal: to explore the unknown space of Chinese environmental NGOs and understand the mechanisms behind the formation of their organizational forms.

Group Composition and Decision-Making on Adopting New Technologies 

(Ongoing) Experimental Design | Consumer Behavior | Statistical Analysis

In many scenarios, users have first exposure to product/service within a group, and thus they make decisions of whether to use or not to use it in a group setting, e.g. buying a car, purchasing a ticket for a new broadway show, etc. Therefore, understanding the decision-making process in a group environment becomes crucial for product design and overall user experience.

In this experimental study, I aim to understand the effect of group composition on group decision making by controlling for the type of members reside in a group. Group members are asked to do a ‚Äútreasure hunt‚Äù of finding a item to purchase on a unfamiliar online shopping site. They have to eventually make a unanimous decision together. Following the Technology Acceptance Model (TAM), perceived usefulness and perceived ease of use are measured by an exit survey at the end of the experiment.

About Me


My name is Licheng. I am a ramen-eating user researcher who likes to solve complex problems. My research approach is always business-aware, where I select and mix up a variety of research methodologies to deliver most effective and efficient solutions.

I ventured into user research from being a passionate sociologist for 6 years in academia. I worked for NGOs, a governmental contractor of polling and a hyper-growing tech startup. Throughout my career, I have always focused on human stories and how they navigate through the fast-changing environments around them.

Capturing and comprehending emotions and motivations is always the center of my work. I find myself incredibly joyful when I connect with people, think from their perspectives, and solve their issues. Interviews are one of my favorite methods of all time, though I also utilize more statistically and computationally-intensive methods to study user types, needs and interactions.

I am also a gamer, a tea-drinker, a technologist, a traveler, a hugger, and a surprisingly good singer in shower.
